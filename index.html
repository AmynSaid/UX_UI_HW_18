My Work

NSA Website ReDesign

Overview:

Understand the pain points within a government website and define the particular design problem that exist . Redesign the NSAs website by using the research, define, ideate, prototype and testing stages of design.

Role & Responsibility

Role: UX Researcher & UI Designer

Responsibility: Develop a redesign website of the NSA's website to meet customer needs and maintain or increase the retention rate.

User Path

My users visit the NSA website to discover cyber threats and how to avoid them to keep privacy rights. Users also visit the site to view the news segment to see the NSA/CSS purpose and discussions with administration or specialists. The NSA/CSS has many components, including cybersecurity, research, and a section for students and educators.

Website/Web Pages

The NSA/CSS web pages include "About Us", "What We Do", "News & Features", "Resources", "Join our Team", and "Doing Business With Us" web pages. Each of those web pages has an in-depth navigation system to many more outlets that they provide.

Wireframes:

Homepage > About Us > Mission Values

Homepage > What We Do > Understanding the Threat

Homepage > News & Features > Digital Media Center > Image Galleries

Homepage > Resources > Student and Educator > K-12 Partnership

Heuristic Evaluation

“The NSA homepage overall was presented well, but navigating from page to page was very complex.”

“NSA failed to realise the value of design”

“Website lacked efficient communication within the design aspect between them and their users.”

“Website needs both information and the investment in visual communication.”

Usability Testing

“This task was hard to find just because they had a student section on the homepage and also in the navigation bars. It was easy to locate, but I was a bit confused on why they had it set up like that.”

“The Conference & Event page is not clear and does not have a clear list of some sort. It takes you to another site which can be misleading for some users.”

Usability Testing

Users performed three simple tasks Navigate to find new information that's released from the NSA. How can you join and be a part of the NSA? Where would you go to learn about the NSA and what they do?

Most users were able to navigate with ease, but did not want just text underneath the eye catching image on the homepage, rather they wanted more things that the NSA has to offer. With that I was able to shift from my wireframes a little to create something the users need in order for them to have the full experience of the NSAs site.

Success Rate: Successful, Half Successful, Failure.

Case Study Conclusion

Research & Problem: The point of this research was based on understanding how people respond to the UI design on a government website of our choice. In this case, this case study was on the National Security Agency (NSA) where I redesigned their site to not only maintain their users, but to also establish user growth and to increase the retention rate. To do that I created a navigation system that works efficiently where users can access what the NSA has to offer on one single homepage.

Goal: The goal for this case study was to increase the interaction with existing and potential users by redesigning the RWD UI style guide that already exist. Another factor that I placed an importance on is the organization of content to increase the effectiveness of the call to action of the page.

Future: For the websites future, I tend to maintain clear navigation from page to page since there are tons of information that are uploaded to the site. Another factor that I want to maintain is having a stable purpose. The main message of the website should not be lost as time goes by when technology advancing by the day.
